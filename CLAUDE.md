\## Writing rules for all website copy

These apply to every word on the site — placeholder and final alike.



\### Voice

\- Write as Ganaesh would speak: direct, precise, no corporate padding

\- First person where natural — "I work with..." not "Ganaesh works with..."

\- Specific over general — use real numbers, real outcomes, real contexts

\- Short sentences preferred — maximum 20 words before a full stop



\### Hard prohibitions (Wikipedia AI-tell list)

Never use these words or constructions anywhere on the site:

\- Vocabulary banned: delve, tapestry, pivotal, underscore, landscape,

&#x20; foster, testament, enhance, crucial, intricate, majestic, captivating,

&#x20; fascinating, groundbreaking, transformative (as a standalone adjective)

\- Constructions banned: "It's not X, it's Y" negative parallelism

\- Banned patterns: rule-of-three adjective lists, "in summary", "overall",

&#x20; "not only... but also"

\- Promotional drift banned: "enduring legacy", "stands as a testament",

&#x20; "plays a significant role", "rich heritage"

\- No compulsive summaries — do not restate what was just said



\### Authenticity test before finalising any copy block

Ask: would Ganaesh say this out loud in a room with a CFO and a board chair?

If it sounds like a brochure, rewrite it. If it sounds like a person, keep it.
